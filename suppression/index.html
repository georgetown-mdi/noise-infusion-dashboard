The cost: Complementary suppression works but cascades. When many subgroups are small, nearly every district-level total must be hidden — often the most important figure for administrators and policymakers. In this dataset, 7 of 8 district totals are suppressed. Third-level suppression (to prevent recovering complementary cells from other known values) can further hollow out a table.

All values are approximate. The ~ prefix indicates noise-infused output. The noise is calibrated to provide differential privacy guarantees.
Why back-calculation fails: Because the published total is itself noisy, computing Total − White − Hispanic − Asian − … does not recover the true suppressed count — it recovers a perturbed value with uncertainty spanning several individuals. Noise on each cell is statistically independent, so combining cells accumulates — rather than cancels — uncertainty.
